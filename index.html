Grand Master Gil

Grand Master Gil has been fascinated with martial arts and has trained and taught for 46 years. His accomplishments include a 6th-degree black belt in Budo-Ryo kempo karate under master t.hings, a 4th-degree black belt in ging Wu Kung Fu under Master Margi Hilbig, a grandmasters 9th-degree black belt level in Rutano Estokada Kali under Grand Master Dan Rutano. He has also trained in the art of Ning Ju Jitsu under Master Dave Machiver and in the art of boxing for 26 years under the direction of Mr. Peter Esterbroos. Grand Master Gil also holds an instructor level in the art of Fan Jeet Kune Do (Bruce Lee system) under the guidance of Grand Master Richard Bustillo.

Over the years Grand Master Gil has won 70 grand championships for kumite (sparring). A true highlight in his career came in 1991 when he won the CanAm world championship for point fighting. In 2000 he made it to the semi-finals in the world full contact stick fighting championship in California. He also won the gold medal in fighting and the silver medal for Kata at the world martial arts games in Las Vegas in 2010.

Grand Master Gil has also felt very fortunate to be able to teach seminars around the world, teaching law enforcement and the military.

GRAND MASTER GIL HAS RECENTLY BEEN INDUCTED INTO THE MARTIAL ARTS HALL OF FAME.

What is Budo Ryu Kempo?

Budo Ryu Kempo has its roots tied strongly to the original precepts of true martial arts. The system is well rounded covering all aspects of martial training. Budo Ryu Kempo will hone your body and your mind, your most important ally. As members become seniors in the grades they will come to realize that Budo Ryu Kempo is truly a way of teaching strong self-discipline, honour, loyalty, integrity, positive thinking and motivation. Good training makes good people. Budo Ryu Kempo training consists of the many elements of martial arts. Your physical fitness will be enhanced as you learn the basics, the building blocks of the arts, kata, and its application, self-defence, free sparring, and weaponry and for those who wish to compete there is specific tournament training. History can be very important to your training. Remember the past lie in the present, and shape your future.
